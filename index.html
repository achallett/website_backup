Do you have any spare change?

Homeless people are seeing a decline in on the street donations due to 2 main reasons:- People are carrying less cash around than ever before and people fear the money may not actually be helping and instead feeding bad habits.

Sorry I don't have any change.

Gyve came about because of the personal experiences of our founders, they noticed the problem of not carrying cash to enable on the spot donations was becoming increasingly common. As they are all in the mobile software space they sought out to build an app that allows users to donate physical items to those in need.

How the Gyve App works.

Donors can use the Gyve mobile app to scan a persons unique QR address and donate a fixed amount. At the time of the donation we find our nearest partner to the person in need. The homeless person will then receive a unique code to the registered phone number to redeem against the goods in a nearby shop. Our partner will enter the code from the homeless person and give them the goods.

Gyve registers Homeless

A Gyve volunteer issues a person in need with a unique QR code.

Donor scans QR

A Gyve user notices a person in need of some food.  They scan the persons QR code do donate food from a nearby vendor.  Payment is taken using Apple pay or Google Pay

Homeless recieves voucher

The person in need recieves a unique code via text message to their registered mobile phone.

Redeems voucher

The person in need enters the Gyve partners food store and shows the text message and the unique code.  Vendor then gyves food to the person in need.

Donor is notified

The original donor is sent a notification via push to let them know the person has food tonight.
